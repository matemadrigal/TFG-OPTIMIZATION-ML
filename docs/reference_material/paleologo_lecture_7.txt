Interpretations of Rademacher Complexity

As the covariance to random noise:  as a random covariate. We can
interpret R as the expected value of the highest covariance of the
performance measure of a strategy to random noise. If, on average, for
every set of +/-1 indicators, there is at least a strategy that
covaries with it, then "we can do no wrong'': for every realization of a
random series, there's a strategy that would do well matching it. If we
interpret the x[t,n] as predictions for epoch t, then this means that
for every sequence of events [t] we have a strategy that predicts them
well.
